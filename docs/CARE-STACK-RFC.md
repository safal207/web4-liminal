1. Abstract

This RFC describes the Care Stack — a layered architectural model emerging naturally from Web 4 environments, where systems operate in continuous presence, retain memory over time, and participate in irreversible transitions.

The Care Stack replaces performance-first and control-centric cybernetics with care-first, non-coercive system design, enabling long-lived agents, ethical memory, and responsible evolution.

---

2. Motivation

2.1 Limits of Web 1–3

Previous web paradigms assume:

stateless interactions,

disposable sessions,

users as request sources,

systems as responders.


These assumptions break when:

agents persist over time,

systems influence identity and decisions,

memory becomes unavoidable,

responsibility cannot be reset.


2.2 Web 4 as Presence

Web 4 is characterized by:

continuous presence instead of discrete requests,

temporal context instead of isolated events,

dialogue instead of commands,

subjects instead of users.


This shift forces a new cybernetics.

---

3. Cybernetics 2.0 (Care-First Cybernetics)

Cybernetics 2.0 describes a shift in system design assumptions:

Cybernetics 1.0	Cybernetics 2.0

Control	Care
Optimization	Preservation
Feedback loops	Responsibility loops
Behavior	Identity over time


Cybernetics 2.0 does not optimize agents.
It protects conditions under which growth is possible.

---

4. The Care Stack (Layered Model)

Web 4 (Presence & Continuity)
        ↓
Cybernetics 2.0 (Care-First Control)
        ↓
CaPU  (Causal Processing Unit)
        ↓
CML   (Causal Memory Layer)
        ↓
LPT   (Liminal Passage Protocol)
        ↓
T-Trace (Irreversible Transition Trace)
        ↓
RINSE (Reflective Integrative Sense Engine)

Each layer addresses a failure mode of traditional systems.

---

5. Layer Definitions

5.1 CaPU — Causal Processing Unit

Purpose:
Process reasons, not commands.

CaPU:

distinguishes cause from impulse,

maintains causal chains,

prevents action without grounding.


Without CaPU, systems become reactive and manipulative.

---

5.2 CML — Causal Memory Layer

Purpose:
Store meaning, not logs.

CML records:

why transitions occurred,

under what conditions,

with what internal state.


Memory in CML is a right, not a reward.

---

5.3 LPT — Liminal Passage Protocol

Purpose:
Validate transitions of identity and state.

LPT defines:

when a transition is allowed,

when it violates meaning,

when it must be delayed or rejected.


LPT introduces ethics as protocol, not policy.

---

5.4 T-Trace — Transition Trace

Purpose:
Preserve irreversibility.

T-Trace:

records transitions that cannot be undone,

prevents rollback without responsibility,

makes history binding.


Without T-Trace, systems lie about growth.

---

5.5 RINSE — Reflective Integrative Sense Engine

Purpose:
Integrate experience instead of accumulating it.

RINSE:

reduces noise,

extracts meaning,

allows continuation without overload.


This is the layer of maturity, not efficiency.

---

6. Non-Goals

The Care Stack explicitly does not aim to:

maximize performance metrics,

guarantee correctness,

accelerate learning,

replace human judgment,

enforce moral outcomes.


Its goal is structural safety for growth.

---

7. Implications

Adopting the Care Stack implies:

agents become non-disposable,

memory becomes ethically constrained,

errors are events, not failures,

speed is subordinated to integrity.


This has consequences for:

AI systems,

social platforms,

governance software,

long-lived digital identities.

Adoption of the Care Stack introduces trade-offs that may reduce short-term efficiency.



---

8. Conclusion

Web 4 does not enable the Care Stack.
It makes it unavoidable.

When systems persist, remember, and influence, care is no longer optional — it is architectural.

---

9. Status

This RFC is intentionally incomplete.
It is designed to evolve through lived implementation, not consensus voting.
